-- The Planning Process --

Importance Of Urban Planning

City planning follows a process that starts with nonspecific ideas and concepts based on research and data gathered,and these ideas slowly become more specific. Eventually, after many stages, a final detailed plan and policies are formed. The planning process can be organized into the following phases:

  • Big Ideas (long-range planning):

    This phase is where planners research and gather data about the current state of an area, including things like population demographics, environmental conditions, existing city services, and infrastructure. The data gathered, in conjunction with other planning documents, help form the overall vision for the area. This phase's resulting plans and policies are high-level but act as blueprints for refinement.

    Some tools/documents used and created in this phase include:

    • The official city plan or Municipal Development Plan

      This formal document guides a city's long-term development, growth and policy decisions. This document is the overall vision of how the city should be developed and is meant to provide the framework for how developments within the city get designed, planned and developed.

    • Inter-municipal Development Plans

      These are documents made by a city in cooperation with neighboring cities to determine how future growth and development in the areas between should be handled/carried out. This document ensures constant and continued cooperative growth and development between the cities/municipalities.

  • Refined plans

    In this phase, more research reflects the overall vision for the area occurs. Data gathered here is then used to build on top of the high-level plans and policies from the last phase, resulting in more detailed plans and policies being created.

    Some tools/documents used and created in this phase include:

    • Area Structure Plans

      The purpose of this document is to provide a detailed framework for the development of a specific large area within a municipality. These documents designate a specific area's land usage, ultimately allowing planners to understand how an area will look when developed.

  • Detailed plans:

    This phase is where the final plan builds upon plans created in the last phase and forms a more detailed/focused plan. This plan is then reviewed and revised, eventually becoming the final plan or policy, which is very detailed and exact.

    Some tools/documents used and created in this phase include:

    • Zoning bylaw

      The purpose of this document is to provide a detailed framework for the development of a specific large area within a municipality. These documents designate a specific area's land usage, ultimately allowing planners to understand how an area will look when developed.

    • Development permits

      Documents which grant permission to property owners or developers to develop an area. These documents ensure that new development follows plans, guidelines, and other documents for developing an area.
